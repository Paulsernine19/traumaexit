Evidence-Based Vehicle Extrication

In this section, we will discuss the extrication of patients in the prehospital setting. This section is based on the EXIT Project — EXtrication In Trauma — conducted by Nutbeam and colleagues between 2021 and 2024. The project reviewed current evidence, analysed trauma registry data, measured spinal movement during different extrication methods, explored patient experience, and produced expert consensus principles for clinical and rescue practice.

Select the title below to learn more about the EXIT Project, then watch the overview video.

The EXIT Project included 10 studies designed to create an evidence base for vehicle extrication. These studies examined what is known, what is assumed, patient outcomes, spinal movement, self-extrication, patient experience, and expert consensus.
